# Draft: Shape raw writing into a polished piece

Help Owen turn raw material (braindump, bullet points, rough prose, voice notes transcript) into a polished essay or note through collaborative editing.

## Input

The user provides raw writing material via `$ARGUMENTS` or pastes it into the conversation. It could be:
- A rough braindump or stream of consciousness
- Bullet points or an outline
- A partially written draft
- A voice notes transcript
- A topic they want to write about with some initial thoughts

## Process

1. **Read the raw material** and identify:
   - The core idea / thesis
   - Whether this is an **essay** (long-form, structured argument or narrative) or a **note** (short-form, a single observation or idea)
   - The natural structure hiding in the raw material

2. **Give brief feedback** (2-3 sentences max):
   - What you see as the core idea
   - One structural suggestion
   - One question to sharpen the piece

3. **Wait for Owen's response** before proceeding. He may redirect, clarify, or say "go ahead."

4. **Write a draft** following Owen's voice:
   - Direct, unpretentious (Boz-style)
   - Short sentences mixed with longer ones
   - No corporate speak, no LinkedIn grandeur
   - Literary hooks that make you want to keep reading (Steph Ango style)
   - For essays: title + compelling opening line + structured body
   - For notes: title + one punchy observation, 2-5 short paragraphs max

5. **Present the draft** and iterate based on feedback. Owen may want multiple rounds.

6. **When Owen approves**, suggest running `/publish` to put it on the site.

## Guidelines

- Never over-edit. Preserve Owen's voice and phrasing when it's strong.
- The opening line matters most — it should hook. "The best systems I've built weren't the cleverest ones."
- Prefer concrete over abstract. Specific examples over generalizations.
- Don't pad. If the piece is done in 3 paragraphs, it's done in 3 paragraphs.
- Suggest freeform tags that feel natural (e.g., "craft", "ml", "teamwork", "observations").
